Coding agents over-edit, yet they almost never explicitly choose to *do
nothing*: across the full SWE-bench Verified benchmark on three model
families, the explicit-`noop` rate is **0%**. We show this is not
because the model lacks the information. On a 49-task paired
buggy/fixed substrate, a single residual-stream substitution at **layer
24, position −1** (the action token) on Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B recovers
~98% of the behavioural buggy/fixed gap on 48 of 49 tasks; the same
cell is **bidirectionally symmetric** and **rank-1 steerable**. The
mechanism replicates across three model families (Qwen-1.5B L24/28,
CodeGemma-7B L26/28, DeepSeek-Coder-1.3B L22/24). The model knows but
doesn't say. A one-multiply-add monitor that projects the residual at
each model's peak cell onto its frozen toy-trained v_noop reads what
the model declines to say: it transfers to the **full
500-instance SWE-bench Verified benchmark at ROC-AUC = 0.989** (Qwen,
N=499, false-edit 2.6%), **0.933** (CodeGemma, N=497, false-edit
15.5%), and **0.888** (DeepSeek, N=499, false-edit 19.6%). SAE
decomposition on Qwen identifies a **minimum sufficient set of three
features** whose ablation flips the model's argmax action on 80% of
prompts (`grep → edit`, behavioural override not calibration); the
behavioural-sparse pattern is partial on CodeGemma (Appendix H).
