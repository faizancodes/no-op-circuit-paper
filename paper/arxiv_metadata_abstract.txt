Language models often represent information they do not act on; we give a clean causal instance in coding agents. In a static, single-turn prompt, a small code model chooses among a fixed five-action menu (view/grep/test/edit/noop) at one action token. Across 499/497/499 SWE-bench-Verified-derived paired prompts, Qwen and CodeGemma never select the single-token no-op action at first-token argmax; DeepSeek also avoids canonical noop under a first-subword proxy, though a single-token rerun reveals a first-position bias. On Qwen, paired residual patching localizes a causally used action-position site (layer 24) whose contrast direction tracks explicit pass/fail transcript evidence and modulates the edit-vs-abstain submargin. Rank-1 steering reproduces the submargin shift, and a 200-pair SWE-derived peak-cell check supports transfer of the effect. A frozen one-dot-product projection separates failing-transcript from passing-transcript prompts at AUC 0.989/0.950/0.888 across Qwen, CodeGemma, and DeepSeek. Controls bound the interpretation: when code and transcript disagree, the projection follows the transcript; on Qwen it is near chance without a transcript; and on clean or noisy pytest-style transcripts, regex or bag-of-words baselines match or beat it. The contribution is mechanistic, not operational: the model carries pass/fail transcript evidence that causally moves an action submargin, while first-token action priors keep abstention noncompetitive. Deployment as an edit veto requires a live multi-step agent-loop study.
